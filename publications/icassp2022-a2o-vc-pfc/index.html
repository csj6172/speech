Improving Intelligibility of Any-to-One Voice Conversion using Phoneme Focal CTC loss

Hyunho Seo, Hanbin Bae, Jae-Sung Bae, Young-Sun Joo, Hoon-Young Cho

hhseo@ncsoft.com


Abstract
     Recently, phonetic posteriorgrams (PPG)-based voice conversion (VC) has
been diversified and improved. The PPG-based VC achieved the similarity and the naturalness in terms of
the conversion of the unknown source speakers. However, PPG-based VC often drops the intelligibility of
converted speech. Because the PPG has the different values between any unseen sources and a target.
we propose a any-to-one VC system that introduces a phoneme focal connectionist temporal classification
(focal CTC) loss function and an auxiliary phoneme classifier (APC). To improve the intelligibility,
i.e., pronunciation accuracy, a phoneme focal CTC loss is adopted, and an APC is introduced.
Because the loss function focus on the reducing the imbalanced problem of the phoneme classes in the APC
and on the reliving the issue of the different values between any unseen source speakers and a target speaker.
Furthermore, for improving to generate the mel-spectrogram of the converted speech, our system applies
a transformer encoder with a windowed attention-based decoder. Because the windowed attention makes the
self-attention focus on the local condition and also requires less computation cost. The qualitative and
quantitative evaluation results prove that our proposed system is more effective than the baseline system.
