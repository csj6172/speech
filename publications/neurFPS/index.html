FastPitch TTS with Extended Pitch Controllability
Using Personlaity-Preserving Pitch Augmentation

Hanbin Bae, Young-Sun Joo, Hoon-Young Cho


Abstract
    Recently, neural text-to-speech models with additional input features have been proposed.
For example, the FastPitch model uses pitch and duration as additional inputs. However, for
pitch values that significantly deviate from the average pitch, the quality of the synthesized
speech is reduced considerably. To address this issue, we propose two algorithms to ensure
the robustness of FastPitch: a personality-preserving pitch augmentation using the neural
formant-preserving pitch shift (neurFPS) algorithm, and a learning algorithm to use pitch-
augmented datasets with different pitch ranges in the same sentence. Our results indicate
that FastPitch when applied with our proposed algorithms ensure pitch controllability within
at least four semitones from the predicted pitch. This clearly improved a pitch controllability
of the baseline FastPitch that can generate natural speech within at most two semitones from
the predicted pitch.
