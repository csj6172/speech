Enhancing Pitch-Controllable Text-to-Speech using
Formant-Preserving Pitch-Shifting Based Pitch Augmentation

Hanbin Bae, Young-Sun Joo, Hoon-Young Cho

bhb0722@ncsoft.com


Abstract
    In this paper, we propose methods for enhancing the robustness of pitch-controllable
text-to-speech (TTS) models. A recently developed pitch-controllable TTS model, e.g. FastPitch,
is additionally conditioned on fundamental frequency contours. However, the quality of the
synthesized speech degrades considerably for pitch values that deviate significantly from the
average pitch. In other words, the ability to control the pitch is limited. To address this
issue, we propose two algorithms to ensure the robustness of pitch-controllable TTS model.
First, we propose the neural formant-preserving pitch-shifting (NeurFPS) algorithm for natural
pitch augmentation. By applying the proposed NeurFPS algorithm, the pitch-shifted speech samples
sound natural because the speaker’s vocal timbre is maintained. We then propose a learning
algorithm for the pitch-controllable TTS model that uses pitch-augmented speech datasets with
different pitch ranges for the same sentence. Experimental results demonstrate that the proposed
algorithms improve the pitch controllability of FastPitch.
